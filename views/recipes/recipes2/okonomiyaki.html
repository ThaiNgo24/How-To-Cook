About the Dish

Origin: Okonomiyaki, meaning “grilled as you like,” originated in Osaka and Hiroshima, evolving into a beloved Japanese comfort food.
Flavor: A savory pancake made with batter, cabbage, and various toppings like meat, seafood, and cheese, topped with okonomiyaki sauce, mayonnaise, and bonito flakes.
Cultural Significance: A symbol of regional pride, Okonomiyaki reflects Japan’s creative and communal dining traditions.

Ingredients

  • For the Batter:
    • 4 bacon slices, sliced (fat & rind removed)
    • 2/3 cup plain flour / all-purpose flour
    • 2 tbsp cornstarch / cornflour
    • 1/2 cup warm water
    • 1 tsp dashi powder dissolved in the warm water
    • 3 eggs
    • 1/4 cabbage wombok / napa – finely shredded
    • 1-2 spring onion / green onion, sliced (half for batter & half for garnish)
    • 1/2 cup corn (tinned)
    • 1 tbsp vegetable oil for cooking
  • Toppings:
    • 2 tbsp kewpie mayonnaise
    • 2 tbsp okonomiyaki sauce
    • 1 tsp seaweed flakes / aonori
    • 1 tsp bonito flakes / katsuobushi

Instructions

  1. Mix flour, cornstarch, water with dissolved dashi, and eggs until smooth.
  2. Fold in cabbage, half the spring onion, corn, and bacon slices.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a pan. Pour batter to form a thick pancake.
  4. Cook on medium heat until golden on both sides and cooked through.
  5. Drizzle with Kewpie mayonnaise and okonomiyaki sauce.
  6. Sprinkle with seaweed flakes, bonito flakes, and remaining spring onion.
