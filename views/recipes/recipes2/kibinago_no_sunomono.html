About the Dish

Origin: Sunomono is a traditional Japanese dish commonly found in many regional cuisines across Japan. It's a popular dish in the summer due to its light and refreshing nature.
Flavor: Sunomono features a tangy, slightly sweet flavor, with the acidity of the vinegar dressing complementing the freshness of the vegetables or seafood.
Cultural Significance: Sunomono is often served at Japanese feasts, casual meals, and festive occasions. It's valued for its refreshing taste and lightness, making it a perfect dish to balance out richer foods.

Ingredients

  • Cucumber: 1, thinly sliced
  • Rice vinegar: 3 tablespoons
  • Soy sauce: 1 tablespoon
  • Sugar: 1 tablespoon
  • Salt: A pinch
  • Wakame (seaweed): 1/4 cup, soaked and chopped (optional)
  • Carrot: 1 small, thinly sliced (optional)
  • Shiso leaves: For garnish (optional)
  • Sesame seeds: For garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Thinly slice the cucumber and carrot (if using). Sprinkle a little salt over the cucumber slices and let them sit for 5 minutes to release excess moisture. Pat them dry with a paper towel.
  2. If using, soak the wakame seaweed in water for about 5-10 minutes, then drain and chop into bite-sized pieces.
  3. In a small bowl, mix the rice vinegar, soy sauce, sugar, and a pinch of salt until the sugar dissolves.
  4. In a larger bowl, combine the cucumber, carrot, and wakame (if using). Pour the vinegar dressing over the vegetables and toss gently to coat evenly.
  5. Let the sunomono marinate for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  6. Serve the sunomono in small bowls, garnished with shiso leaves and sesame seeds, if desired. Enjoy as a light and refreshing side dish!
