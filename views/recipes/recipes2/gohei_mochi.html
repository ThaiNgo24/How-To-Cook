About the Dish

Origin: Gohei Mochi, a traditional snack from central Japan, has been enjoyed for centuries during festivals in rural areas.
Flavor: The nutty sweetness of miso sauce, enhanced by grilling, pairs with the chewy rice cake.
Cultural Significance: A symbol of rural Japan, Gohei Mochi reflects the spirit of simple, communal festivities.

Ingredients

  • Walnut Miso Paste:
    • 1 tbsp Miso
    • 1 1/2 tbsp Raw Sugar
    • 1 tbsp Mirin or Sake
    • 20g Roasted Walnuts (0.7 oz)
    • 1/2 tbsp Toasted White Sesame Seeds
  • Toppings:
    • Toasted White Sesame Seeds
    • Sansho Pepper or Shichimi Chili Powder - optional
  • 5 Wooden Popsicle Sticks
  • Aluminum Foil
  • Vegetable Oil

Instructions

  1. Grind roasted walnuts and toasted sesame seeds into a fine paste.
  2. Mix with miso, raw sugar, and mirin (or sake) until smooth. Set aside.
  3. Shape cooked rice into flat ovals or rounds and insert popsicle sticks.
  4. Wrap sticks in aluminum foil to prevent burning.
  5. Lightly brush mochi with vegetable oil and grill on both sides until lightly golden.
  6. Brush grilled mochi with walnut miso paste.
  7. Return to the grill and cook briefly until the paste caramelizes.
  8. Top with sesame seeds and sprinkle Sansho or Shichimi if desired.
