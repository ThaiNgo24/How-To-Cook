About the Dish

Origin: Yudofu is a simple tofu hot pot from Kyoto, traditionally enjoyed in Zen Buddhist temples.
Flavor: Silken tofu simmered in a light kombu broth, served with soy sauce or ponzu for a delicate, clean taste.
Cultural Significance: A symbol of Kyoto’s refined and minimalist cuisine, Yudofu reflects the principles of Zen Buddhism and seasonal eating.

Ingredients

  • Silken tofu: 1 block, cut into large cubes
  • Kombu (dried kelp): 1 piece (about 10 cm long)
  • Water: 4 cups
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons (for dipping)
  • Ponzu sauce: Optional (for added flavor)
  • Chopped green onions: For garnish
  • Grated ginger: For serving
  • Bonito flakes (katsuobushi): Optional, for added umami

Instructions

  1. Place the kombu in a pot with water and let it soak for at least 30 minutes. Heat gently until just before boiling, then remove the kombu.
  2. Add the silken tofu cubes to the kombu broth. Heat gently until the tofu is warmed through (do not boil).
  3. Place the tofu cubes in serving bowls. Garnish with green onions, grated ginger, and bonito flakes if using.
  4. Serve with soy sauce or ponzu sauce on the side for dipping.
