About the Dish

Origin: Nizakana is a traditional Japanese dish found in various regional cuisines, where fish is simmered in a flavorful broth. It is commonly served in home kitchens and restaurants across Japan.
Flavor: The fish absorbs the savory and slightly sweet flavors of the simmering broth, creating a rich and tender dish that pairs perfectly with rice.
Cultural Significance: Nizakana is a beloved comfort food in Japanese households, often eaten as part of a meal with rice and side dishes.

Ingredients

  • White fish (e.g., mackerel, cod, or sea bass): 2 pieces (or a whole fish, cut into fillets)
  • Soy sauce: 3 tablespoons
  • Mirin: 3 tablespoons
  • Sake: 2 tablespoons
  • Sugar: 1 tablespoon (adjust to taste)
  • Ginger: 2-3 slices (optional, for added flavor)
  • Green onions: For garnish (optional)
  • Water: 1 cup

Instructions

  1. In a saucepan, combine soy sauce, mirin, sake, sugar, and water. Stir to dissolve the sugar and bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat.
  2. Once the broth is simmering, carefully add the fish pieces to the pot, skin-side down.
  3. Add the ginger slices if using, and let the fish simmer for 10-15 minutes, spooning the broth over the fish occasionally to ensure it’s evenly flavored.
  4. Once the fish is tender and cooked through, remove it from the pot and plate it.
  5. Reduce the sauce slightly if desired and pour it over the fish before serving. Garnish with chopped green onions if desired.
  6. Serve the nizakana with a bowl of rice and enjoy!
