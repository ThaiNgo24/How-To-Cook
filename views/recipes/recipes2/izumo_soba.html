About the Dish

Origin: Izumo Soba hails from the Izumo region of Shimane Prefecture in Japan, known for its high-quality buckwheat production.
Flavor: The noodles have a hearty, nutty flavor due to the use of 100% buckwheat flour. It is often served with a flavorful broth or dipping sauce that complements the earthiness of the noodles.
Cultural Significance: Izumo Soba is a beloved regional dish, often enjoyed during special occasions and festivals. It is also considered a comfort food in the area.

Ingredients

    • Izumo soba noodles: 200g (or any buckwheat soba noodles)
    • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
    • Mirin: 2 tablespoons
    • Bonito dashi stock: 2 cups (or use vegetable broth as a substitute)
    • Sugar: 1 teaspoon
    • Green onions: Chopped, for garnish
    • Wasabi: For dipping (optional)
    • Tempura (optional): For serving with soba

Instructions

  1. Prepare the dashi stock by heating water and adding bonito flakes, letting it simmer for about 10 minutes. Strain the stock to remove the flakes and return the clear broth to the pot.
  2. Add soy sauce, mirin, and sugar to the dashi stock and bring it to a simmer, adjusting the seasoning to taste.
  3. Cook the soba noodles according to the package instructions, then rinse them under cold water to remove excess starch.
  4. Serve the noodles either by pouring the hot broth over them (for a hot soba dish) or by serving the broth in a separate bowl as a dipping sauce (for cold soba).
  5. Garnish with chopped green onions and serve with tempura or wasabi for added flavor, if desired.
