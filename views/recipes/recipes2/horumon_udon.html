About the Dish

Origin: Horumon Udon is a regional dish, particularly popular in Osaka and other parts of Western Japan, where horumon (offal) is commonly used in various dishes.
Flavor: The dish combines the chewy texture of udon noodles with the rich, savory flavor of stir-fried horumon, creating a satisfying and hearty meal. The sauce adds an extra umami punch to the overall dish.
Cultural Significance: Horumon Udon is often enjoyed in casual dining spots like izakayas or specialty restaurants, where it's served as a filling and flavorful comfort food.

Ingredients

  • Udon noodles: 200g (fresh or frozen)
  • Horumon (pork or beef offal): 150g, cleaned and chopped
  • Onion: 1, thinly sliced
  • Green onion: 2 stalks, chopped
  • Bell pepper: 1, sliced (optional)
  • Ginger: 1 teaspoon, grated
  • Garlic: 2 cloves, minced
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Sake: 1 tablespoon
  • Mirin: 1 tablespoon
  • Oyster sauce: 1 tablespoon (optional, for extra depth)
  • Sesame oil: 1 teaspoon
  • Chili flakes: A pinch (optional, for spiciness)
  • Salt and pepper: To taste

Instructions

  1. Cook the udon noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat sesame oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat. Add the horumon (offal) and stir-fry until it begins to brown and crisp slightly, about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Add the onion, garlic, ginger, and bell pepper (if using) to the pan. Stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes until the vegetables are softened.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the soy sauce, sake, mirin, and oyster sauce (if using). Pour this sauce mixture over the stir-fried horumon and vegetables, and toss to coat everything evenly.
  5. Add the cooked udon noodles to the pan and toss them with the horumon and vegetables. Stir-fry for 1-2 minutes, allowing the noodles to absorb the sauce and heat through.
  6. Season with salt, pepper, and chili flakes (if desired) to taste. Garnish with chopped green onions before serving.
