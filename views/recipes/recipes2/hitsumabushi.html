About the Dish

Origin: Hitsumabushi originated in Nagoya in the late 19th century, offering an affordable eel dish for working-class diners.
Flavor: Smoky, caramelized eel with a soy-based sauce, enjoyed plain, with condiments, or in broth.
Cultural Significance: A dish showcasing Nagoya's culinary ingenuity, highlighting eel in a unique, multi-layered dining experience.

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp turbinado sugar (zarame) - light brown with coarse texture
  • 1 tsp dark brown sugar
  • 75 ml sake
  • 75 ml mirin
  • 75 ml soy sauce
  • 300 g filleted eels (1 fillet is approx 150g)
  • 2 tbsp sake
  • 2 portions cooked Japanese short-grain rice
  • Ground Japanese pepper (sansho) - optional

Instructions

  1. In a saucepan, mix zarame, dark brown sugar, sake, mirin, and soy sauce. Simmer until slightly thickened.
  2. Brush the eel fillets with sake, grill or broil for 3-4 minutes on each side.
  3. Brush the eels with the prepared sauce and grill for another 1-2 minutes until caramelized.
  4. Place cooked rice in bowls, top with sliced eel, and drizzle with extra sauce.
  5. Sprinkle with sansho pepper if desired and enjoy!
