About the Dish

Origin: Osaka and Kyoto.
Flavor: Soft udon in a light dashi broth with sweet, savory fried tofu.
Cultural Significance: Associated with fox legends in Japan, as foxes are thought to love tofu.

Ingredients

  • 2 frozen Udon noodles *I used TableMark (Katokichi), any brand is OK
  • 2 Aburaage (deep-fried Tofu pouch)
  • 100ml Dashi broth (using packaged dashi powder saves time)
  • 1 tbsp. Mirin (sweet Sake)
  • 1 tbsp. sugar
  • 1 tbsp. soy sauce
  • Kaketsuyu (Udon soup):
  • 500ml Dashi broth (using packaged dashi powder saves time)
  • 2 tbsp. Mirin (sweet Sake)
  • 1 tsp. soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp. salt
  • chopped green onions
  • Shichimi-togarashi (seven flavor chili pepper) if you like

Instructions

  1. Cook the udon noodles according to package instructions. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the aburaage by simmering it in a mixture of dashi, mirin, sugar, and soy sauce.
  3. Make the udon soup by combining dashi, mirin, soy sauce, and salt. Heat until warm.
  4. Serve the cooked noodles in a bowl, pour the hot soup over them, and top with prepared aburaage.
  5. Garnish with chopped green onions and shichimi-togarashi if desired.
