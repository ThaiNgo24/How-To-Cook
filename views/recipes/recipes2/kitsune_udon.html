About the Dish

Origin: Kitsune Udon, meaning “fox udon,” originated in Osaka and has become a popular noodle dish across Japan.
Flavor: Thick udon noodles in a light, savory dashi broth, topped with sweet, fried tofu (aburaage) that soaks up the flavors.
Cultural Significance: The dish is named after foxes, who are said to love aburaage in Japanese folklore, adding a playful cultural touch.

Ingredients

  • 2 frozen Udon noodles
  • 2 Aburaage (deep-fried Tofu pouch)
  • 100ml Dashi broth (using packaged dashi powder saves time)
  • 1 tbsp. Mirin (sweet Sake)
  • 1 tbsp. sugar
  • 1 tbsp. soy sauce
  • Kaketsuyu (Udon soup):
  • 500ml Dashi broth (using packaged dashi powder saves time)
  • 2 tbsp. Mirin (sweet Sake)
  • 1 tsp. soy sauce
  • 1/2 tsp. salt
  • chopped green onions
  • Shichimi-togarashi (seven flavor chili pepper) if you like

Instructions

  1. Simmer Aburaage with Dashi broth, mirin, sugar, and soy sauce (ingredients in "A") for 10 minutes. Set aside.
  2. Boil the frozen Udon noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. Heat Dashi broth, mirin, soy sauce, and salt for the Udon soup until warm.
  4. Place Udon noodles in a bowl, pour the hot soup over them.
  5. Top with seasoned tofu, green onions, and sprinkle Shichimi-togarashi if desired.
