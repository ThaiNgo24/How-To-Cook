About the Dish

Origin: Okonomiyaki Hiroshima comes from Hiroshima, Japan, and is a regional variation of the classic okonomiyaki dish, famous for its unique layering technique.
Flavor: The dish has a savory, umami-packed flavor with layers of crispy noodles, tender pork, fresh cabbage, and a sweet-savory okonomiyaki sauce.
Cultural Significance: This version of okonomiyaki is a beloved local specialty in Hiroshima, often served as a hearty meal at casual dining spots and food stalls.

Ingredients

  • Flour: 1 cup (for the batter)
  • Eggs: 2
  • Water: 3/4 cup (for the batter)
  • Shredded cabbage: 2 cups
  • Cooked yakisoba noodles: 2 servings
  • Pork belly slices: 4-6 slices
  • Okonomiyaki sauce: 3-4 tablespoons (or substitute with Worcestershire sauce)
  • Mayonnaise: 2 tablespoons (optional, for topping)
  • Green onions: Chopped, for garnish
  • Dried bonito flakes: For garnish
  • Seaweed flakes (aonori): For garnish (optional)
  • Oil: For cooking

Instructions

  1. Mix flour, water, and eggs in a bowl to create a batter. Set aside.
  2. Heat a griddle or large pan and lightly oil the surface.
  3. Pour half of the batter onto the griddle and spread it into a thin circle, similar to a pancake.
  4. Layer the shredded cabbage on top of the batter, followed by a few slices of pork belly and a portion of yakisoba noodles.
  5. Pour the remaining batter over the cabbage and noodles, pressing gently to form a thick pancake.
  6. Cook the okonomiyaki for about 5-7 minutes on each side, flipping carefully to ensure both sides are golden and crispy.
  7. Once cooked, drizzle okonomiyaki sauce on top, and garnish with mayonnaise, green onions, bonito flakes, and aonori if desired.
  8. Serve hot and enjoy this layered, savory pancake!
