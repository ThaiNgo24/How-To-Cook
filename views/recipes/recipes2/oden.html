About the Dish

Origin: Oden is a traditional Japanese dish that has been enjoyed for centuries. It is particularly popular in the winter months across Japan.
Flavor: The dish features a rich, savory broth with various ingredients that absorb the umami flavor from the dashi and soy sauce.
Cultural Significance: Oden is a beloved comfort food, often enjoyed with family or shared at festivals. It’s also common in convenience stores as a quick and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

  • Daikon (Japanese radish): 1/2, cut into thick slices
  • Konjac (konnyaku): 1 block, cut into triangles or squares
  • Boiled eggs: 4, peeled
  • Tofu: 1 block, cut into large cubes
  • Chikuwa (fish cake): 2-3 sticks, cut into pieces
  • Korean-style fish cakes or satsuma-age: 2-3 pieces
  • Soy sauce: 3 tablespoons
  • Mirin: 2 tablespoons
  • Dash (dashi stock): 4 cups
  • Salt: 1 teaspoon

Instructions

  1. Prepare the dashi stock by simmering kombu (kelp) and bonito flakes in water for 10 minutes. Strain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, add the dashi stock, soy sauce, mirin, and salt. Bring it to a boil and then lower the heat to a simmer.
  3. Add the daikon, konjac, tofu, and fish cakes into the pot. Let them simmer gently for about 30-40 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  4. After 30 minutes, add the boiled eggs and continue simmering for another 15-20 minutes, making sure all the ingredients are tender and flavorful.
  5. Serve hot, and enjoy the dish with mustard or a dipping sauce on the side if desired.
