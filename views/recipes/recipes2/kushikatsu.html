About the Dish

Origin: Kushikatsu originated in Osaka, Japan, and has since become a beloved dish throughout the country, especially in street food stalls.
Flavor: A crispy, savory exterior with tender fillings inside, complemented by a tangy dipping sauce.
Cultural Significance: Kushikatsu is often enjoyed in casual settings, typically paired with beer or sake, making it a favorite in Osaka's vibrant food scene.

Ingredients

  • Chicken breast or pork (sliced into bite-sized pieces)
  • Shrimp (peeled and deveined)
  • Vegetables (e.g., onion, bell peppers, eggplant, mushrooms)
  • Breadcrumbs (panko)
  • Flour (for dredging)
  • Egg (for coating)
  • Vegetable oil (for deep-frying)
  • Kushikatsu dipping sauce (or tonkatsu sauce)
  • Skewers (wooden or metal)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the ingredients by cutting the meat and vegetables into bite-sized pieces and skewering them.
  2. Coat the ingredients first in flour, then dip them in beaten egg, and finally coat them with panko breadcrumbs.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large pot to 350°F (175°C). Fry the skewers in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes.
  4. Drain the fried skewers on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  5. Serve the kushikatsu hot with dipping sauce on the side, and enjoy!
