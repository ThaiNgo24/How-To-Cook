About the Dish

Origin: A Nagoya specialty from the early 20th century, featuring a bold red miso sauce on tonkatsu.
Flavor: Crispy pork cutlet with a thick, slightly sweet, umami-rich miso sauce, served with cabbage and rice.
Cultural Significance: A comfort food in Nagoya, showcasing the region’s Hatcho miso.

Ingredients

  • Tonkatsu Sauce:
    • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
    • 1 tbsp tomato ketchup
    • 1 tbsp soy sauce
    • 1 pinch brown sugar
    • 1 tbsp white sesame seeds
  • Tonkatsu:
    • 2 thick boneless pork chops - or similar
    • 1 pinch salt and pepper
    • Cooking oil - for deep frying
    • All-purpose flour - for dusting
    • 1 medium egg
    • 1 tbsp whole milk
    • 1 tsp cooking oil
    • 100 g panko breadcrumbs
  • Serve with (optional):
    • 2 portions cooked Japanese short-grain rice
    • 200 g green cabbage - shredded
    • 2 slices lemon - to garnish

Instructions

  1. Mix miso sauce ingredients in a saucepan, heat until thickened.
  2. Mix tonkatsu sauce ingredients, add toasted sesame seeds.
  3. Season pork, coat in flour, egg mixture, and breadcrumbs.
  4. Deep fry pork at 170–180°C until golden and cooked through.
  5. Slice pork, top with miso sauce.
  6. Serve with rice, cabbage, and lemon if desired.
