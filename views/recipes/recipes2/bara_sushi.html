About the Dish

Origin: Bara Sushi is commonly found in the Kansai region, particularly in Osaka and Kyoto, where it is often served as a celebratory dish.
Flavor: The dish offers a combination of flavors, with the vinegared sushi rice providing a tangy base, topped with fresh fish, seafood, and vegetables that add sweetness, crunch, and umami.
Cultural Significance: Bara Sushi is a popular dish for special occasions such as parties, weddings, and New Year's celebrations in Japan. It’s a more relaxed and festive alternative to traditional sushi rolls.

Ingredients

  • Sushi rice: 2 cups, cooked and seasoned with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt
  • Salmon: 100g, sliced thinly
  • Tuna: 100g, sliced thinly
  • Cooked shrimp: 100g, peeled and sliced in half
  • Avocado: 1, sliced
  • Cucumber: 1, julienned
  • Pickled ginger: For garnish
  • Shiso leaves: For garnish (optional)
  • Sesame seeds: For garnish (optional)
  • Soy sauce: For dipping

Instructions

  1. Prepare the sushi rice by cooking it according to the package instructions. Once cooked, season with rice vinegar, sugar, and salt, and let it cool to room temperature.
  2. Slice the salmon, tuna, and avocado thinly. Julienne the cucumber and prepare any other toppings you want to use, such as pickled ginger or shiso leaves.
  3. Spread the cooled sushi rice evenly in a shallow dish or individual serving bowls.
  4. Arrange the fish, shrimp, avocado, cucumber, and other toppings artfully over the rice in a scattered or decorative pattern.
  5. Garnish with sesame seeds, pickled ginger, and shiso leaves for added flavor and color.
  6. Serve with soy sauce on the side for dipping, and enjoy immediately!
