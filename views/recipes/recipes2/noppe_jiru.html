About the Dish

Origin: A traditional dish from Niigata Prefecture, often enjoyed in the winter months.
Flavor: A hearty soup made with vegetables, tofu, and miso, offering a comforting, savory taste.
Cultural Significance: Noppe Jiru represents the region's agricultural heritage and is commonly served during New Year's celebrations.

Ingredients

  • Walnut Miso Paste:
    • 1 tbsp Miso
    • 1 1/2 tbsp Raw Sugar
    • 1 tbsp Mirin or Sake
    • 20g Roasted Walnuts (0.7 oz)
    • 1/2 tbsp Toasted White Sesame Seeds
  • Toppings:
    • Toasted White Sesame Seeds
    • Sansho Pepper or Shichimi Chili Powder - optional
  • 5 Wooden Popsicle Sticks
  • Aluminum Foil
  • Vegetable Oil

Instructions

  1. Soak dried shiitake mushrooms in water until soft, then slice.
  2. Peel and cut taro, carrot, and cabbage into bite-sized pieces.
  3. Slice konjac and chicken thigh into small pieces.
  4. Blanch snow peas, then cut them diagonally in half.
  5. In a pot, bring 500g water and dashi stock to a boil.
  6. Add chicken, taro, carrot, konjac, cabbage, and mushrooms. Simmer until tender.
  7. Stir in sake, mirin, soy sauce, and salt. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  8. Dissolve potato starch in a small amount of water and gradually add to the soup while stirring.
