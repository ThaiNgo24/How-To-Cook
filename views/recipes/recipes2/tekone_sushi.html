About the Dish

Origin: This rustic sushi dish comes from Ise, Mie Prefecture, where fishermen originally prepared it on boats using freshly caught tuna.
Flavor: Marinated in soy sauce and mirin, the tuna has a sweet and salty umami flavor that pairs beautifully with vinegared rice. The dish is garnished with fresh herbs and sesame for added aroma.
Cultural Significance: Tekone Sushi captures the simplicity and resourcefulness of fishing communities, making it a regional treasure.

Ingredients

  • Tekone Zushi (for 1 serving)
  • 100g bonito Tataki
  • 230g sushi rice
  • Some Kizami Nori
  • Some Kinshi Tamago
  • Some Gari
  • Sushi rice (for 3 servings)
  • 2 cups rice (300g)
  • 5 Shiso leaves
  • 1 Japanese ginger
  • Some Irigoma
  • 50 ml vinegar
  • 1.5 tbsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3 tbsp marinade
  • Marinade (for 3 servings)
  • 3 tbsp sake
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 1.5 tbsp soy sauce
  • Gari (200g)
  • 200g spring ginger
  • 100 ml vinegar
  • 25g sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Instructions

  1. Simmer sake, mirin, and soy sauce until slightly thickened. Set aside to cool.
  2. Cook the rice and mix with vinegar, sugar, and salt while still warm.
  3. Add shredded Shiso leaves, finely chopped Japanese ginger, and Irigoma. Mix well.
  4. Thinly slice spring ginger, boil briefly, and drain.
  5. Mix vinegar, sugar, and salt, then pour over the ginger. Let marinate for 1-2 hours.
  6. Marinate bonito Tataki in the marinade for 5-10 minutes.
  7. Layer sushi rice in a bowl, top with marinated bonito, Kizami Nori, Kinshi Tamago, and Gari.
