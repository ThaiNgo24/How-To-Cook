About the Dish

Origin: Takoyaki originated in Osaka in the 1930s, quickly becoming a popular street food across Japan.
Flavor: The dough is crispy on the outside, soft on the inside, with a savory octopus filling, topped with takoyaki sauce, bonito flakes, and seaweed.
Cultural Significance: Takoyaki is a beloved snack, often enjoyed at festivals, showcasing Osaka's vibrant food culture.

Ingredients

  • SAUCE
    • 1/2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
    • 1/2 tbsp tsuyu sauce (or soy sauce with a bit of sugar)
    • 1/2 tbsp tomato ketchup
    • 1 tbsp honey
  • BATTER
    • 500 ml dashi stock cooled
    • 1 medium egg
    • 100 g cake flour
    • 1 tsp tsuyu sauce (or soy sauce)
    • Sesame oil for cooking
  • FILLINGS
    • 120 g boiled octopus
    • 4 tbsp red pickled ginger (benishoga)
    • 50 g green onion chopped
    • 30 g tempura flakes (tenkasu)
  • TOPPINGS (ADD ACCORDING TO PREFERENCE)
    • Japanese mayonnaise
    • Dried green laver powder (aonori)
    • Bonito flakes (katsuobushi)

Instructions

  1. Mix all sauce ingredients and set aside.
  2. Whisk dashi, egg, cake flour, and tsuyu sauce until smooth.
  3. Heat a takoyaki pan with sesame oil. Pour batter into the molds, filling completely.
  4. Add octopus, ginger, green onion, and tempura flakes into each mold.
  5. Cook until the bottom is golden, then flip and cook evenly into a ball shape.
  6. Top with sauce, mayonnaise, aonori, and bonito flakes.
