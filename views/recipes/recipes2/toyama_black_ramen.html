About the Dish

Origin: Toyama Black Ramen originates from Toyama Prefecture, known for its distinctive dark broth made from soy sauce and pork stock.
Flavor: The broth is rich and savory, with a bold, umami-packed taste. The noodles are typically served with slices of pork, green onions, and bamboo shoots.
Cultural Significance: Toyama Black Ramen is a beloved local specialty, representing the region’s rich culinary history and unique twist on traditional ramen.

Ingredients

  • Ramen noodles: 2 servings
  • Pork broth: 4 cups (made with pork bones and soy sauce)
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Chicken stock: 1 cup
  • Sliced pork belly: 2-3 slices
  • Green onions: 2 stalks, chopped
  • Garlic: 1 clove, minced
  • Sesame seeds: A sprinkle (optional)
  • Pickled bamboo shoots (menma): A few pieces
  • Nori (seaweed): A sheet (optional)
  • Boiled egg: 1, halved

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Broth: In a pot, combine the pork broth, chicken stock, soy sauce, and garlic. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend.
  2. Cook the Noodles: Boil the ramen noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. Assemble the Ramen: Place the cooked noodles in a bowl. Pour the hot broth over the noodles.
  4. Add Toppings: Top with sliced pork belly, green onions, pickled bamboo shoots, a boiled egg, and nori. Sprinkle with sesame seeds if desired.
  5. Serve: Serve hot, and enjoy the deep, savory flavor of Toyama Black Ramen.
