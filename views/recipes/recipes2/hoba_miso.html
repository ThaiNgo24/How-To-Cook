About the Dish

Origin: Hoba Miso comes from the Hida region, specifically Gifu Prefecture in Japan, and is a popular dish enjoyed in the colder months.
Flavor: The miso paste, when grilled on a magnolia leaf, imparts a smoky, umami-rich flavor that pairs beautifully with the savory taste of the miso.
Cultural Significance: Hoba Miso is a comforting, rustic dish often served in traditional Japanese inns or during festivals in the Hida region.

Ingredients

  • Miso paste: 3 tablespoons (preferably red or white miso)
  • Sake: 1 tablespoon
  • Mirin: 1 tablespoon
  • Sugar: 1 teaspoon
  • Tofu: 1 block, cut into slices (optional)
  • Thinly sliced vegetables: such as shiitake mushrooms, eggplant, or zucchini
  • Magnolia leaves (or substitute with banana leaves or foil)
  • Green onions: For garnish

Instructions

  1. Mix miso paste, sake, mirin, and sugar in a bowl to make the miso sauce.
  2. Prepare a magnolia leaf by washing it and placing it on a grill or stovetop.
  3. Spread a thin layer of the miso sauce on the leaf. Arrange slices of tofu and vegetables on top of the miso.
  4. Grill the leaf on medium heat for 5-10 minutes, until the miso is bubbling and the tofu and vegetables are cooked through.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with chopped green onions, and enjoy with rice or as a side dish.
