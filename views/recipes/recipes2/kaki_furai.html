About the Dish

Origin: Kaki Furai is a popular dish in Japan, particularly enjoyed in the winter months when oysters are in season. It is a variant of the western-style breaded and fried foods, adapted with Japanese flavors.
Flavor: The crispy, golden breadcrumb coating contrasts perfectly with the tender, briny oyster inside, often complemented by a tangy sauce or lemon.
Cultural Significance: Kaki Furai is often served in Japanese homes, restaurants, and izakayas (Japanese pubs), typically as part of a larger meal with rice and side dishes.

Ingredients

  • Fresh oysters: 10-12, shucked and drained
  • Flour: 1/2 cup (for dredging)
  • Eggs: 2, beaten (for coating)
  • Panko breadcrumbs: 1 cup (for a crispy coating)
  • Vegetable oil: For deep-frying
  • Salt: To taste
  • Pepper: To taste
  • Lemon: For serving (optional)
  • Tonkatsu sauce or Worcestershire sauce: For dipping (optional)

Instructions

  1. Season the oysters with a pinch of salt and pepper.
  2. Prepare three shallow plates: one with flour, one with beaten eggs, and one with panko breadcrumbs.
  3. Dredge each oyster in the flour, coating it lightly. Then dip it into the beaten egg, followed by a coat of panko breadcrumbs. Press gently to ensure the breadcrumbs stick well.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or fryer to about 350°F (175°C). Fry the oysters in batches for 2-3 minutes, or until they are golden brown and crispy on the outside.
  5. Drain the fried oysters on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  6. Serve hot with lemon wedges and tonkatsu sauce or Worcestershire sauce for dipping.
