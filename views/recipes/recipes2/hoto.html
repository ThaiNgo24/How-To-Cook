About the Dish

Origin: Hōtō is a traditional dish from Yamanashi Prefecture, dating back to the Sengoku period. It was said to be a favorite of samurai general Takeda Shingen, offering a simple yet nutritious meal for warriors.
Flavor: The sweet pumpkin flavor blends with umami-rich miso broth, while thick, chewy noodles add rustic charm.
Cultural Significance: Hōtō is considered soul food in Yamanashi, reflecting its agricultural roots and is especially enjoyed in the cooler months.

Ingredients

  • 300-400g of hoto noodles (or tagliatelle)
  • 200g of thinly sliced pork
  • 1/4 kabocha
  • 1/8 of a napa cabbage
  • 1/2 carrots
  • 1/4 daikon
  • 4-5 shiitake mushrooms or ½ bunch of shimeji
  • 1 long green onion
  • 2 abura-age (fried tofu)
  • 7 cups water
  • 1 tbsp of dashi*
  • 2 tbsp of miso*
  • For the topping: Shichimi (seven-spice)*

Instructions

  1. Cut vegetables, mushrooms, and abura-age into bite-sized pieces. Season pork lightly.
  2. Bring 7 cups of water to a boil, dissolve dashi in it.
  3. Add kabocha, carrot, daikon, and cabbage to the pot, cook for 5-7 minutes.
  4. Add pork, mushrooms, and abura-age, simmer for another 5 minutes.
  5. Add hoto noodles, cook until tender (7-10 minutes).
  6. Dissolve miso in a small amount of soup, mix it back into the pot without boiling.
  7. Top with green onions and serve with shichimi if desired.
