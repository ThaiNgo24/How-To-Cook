About the Dish

Origin: Sabazushi is a traditional sushi from the Kansai region, made with mackerel (saba) and vinegared rice.
Flavor: The mackerel, pickled in vinegar, has a rich, tangy taste that pairs perfectly with the slightly sweet and sour rice.
Cultural Significance: A regional specialty, Sabazushi showcases the preservation techniques of the area, often enjoyed during festivals or special occasions.

Ingredients

  • Mackerel fillets (saba): 2 fillets, deboned
  • Sushi rice: 2 cups, cooked and seasoned with sushi vinegar
  • Sushi vinegar: 1/4 cup (for the rice)
  • Rice vinegar: 1/4 cup (for marinating the mackerel)
  • Salt: 1 tablespoon
  • Kombu (dried kelp): 1 small piece (optional, for extra flavor)
  • Shiso leaves (perilla): A few leaves (optional, for garnish)
  • Wasabi: To taste

Instructions

  1. Sprinkle the mackerel fillets with salt and let them rest for 30 minutes to remove excess moisture.
  2. Rinse the salt off the fillets and marinate them in rice vinegar for 20-30 minutes.
  3. Prepare sushi rice by mixing the cooked rice with sushi vinegar.
  4. Place a layer of sushi rice in a mold (or shape by hand), pressing gently to flatten it.
  5. Lay the marinated mackerel fillet on top of the rice, pressing it down to form a tight layer.
  6. Optionally, wrap the sushi in kombu to enhance flavor and let it rest for 10-15 minutes.
  7. Slice the sabazushi into pieces and serve with wasabi, shiso leaves, or pickled ginger.
