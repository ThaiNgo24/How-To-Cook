About the Dish

Origin: Sanuki Udon comes from Kagawa Prefecture on Shikoku Island, Japan, and is known for its chewy texture.
Flavor: The noodles are firm and chewy, served in a savory broth made from soy sauce, mirin, and dashi, with various toppings like tempura or kamaboko.
Cultural Significance: A beloved comfort food in Japan, Sanuki Udon is particularly popular in Kagawa, where it is considered a regional specialty.

Ingredients

  • Sanuki Udon noodles: 200g (or any thick udon noodles if not available)
  • Water: 4 cups (for the broth)
  • Soy sauce: 3 tablespoons
  • Mirin: 2 tablespoons
  • Dashi: 1 tablespoon (or dashi powder for a quick solution)
  • Green onions: 2 stalks, chopped
  • Tempura (shrimp or vegetables, optional): 2-3 pieces
  • Kamaboko (fish cake, optional): 2-3 slices
  • Sesame seeds: For garnish (optional)
  • Shichimi Togarashi (optional): For spice

Instructions

  1. Cook the Sanuki Udon noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and rinse the noodles under cold water to remove excess starch and prevent sticking.
  2. To make the broth, combine the water, soy sauce, mirin, and dashi in a pot. Bring to a gentle boil over medium heat, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 5 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  3. Once the broth is ready, pour it into bowls.
  4. Add the cooked udon noodles to the broth. If you're serving the noodles cold, arrange the noodles in a separate dish and serve the broth as a dipping sauce (known as "Zaru Udon").
  5. Garnish with chopped green onions, tempura (if using), kamaboko, sesame seeds, and a sprinkle of shichimi togarashi for added flavor and spice.
  6. Serve immediately and enjoy the chewy texture of Sanuki Udon with the savory broth!
