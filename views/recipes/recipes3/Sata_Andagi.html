About the Dish

Origin: Sata Andagi is a traditional sweet from Okinawa, Japan, known for its crispy exterior and soft, fluffy interior.
Flavor: The doughnuts have a mild sweetness with a rich, tender texture, often enjoyed as a snack or dessert.
Cultural Significance: Sata Andagi is a beloved treat in Okinawa, commonly enjoyed at festivals, celebrations, or as a simple snack.

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour: 2 cups
  • Sugar: 1/2 cup
  • Eggs: 2, beaten
  • Milk: 1/4 cup
  • Baking powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Vanilla extract (optional): 1 teaspoon
  • Salt: 1/4 teaspoon
  • Vegetable oil: For deep frying

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt together.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk the eggs, milk, and vanilla extract (if using) until well combined.
  3. Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients, stirring until a thick dough forms. The dough should be firm but sticky.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or pot over medium heat to about 350°F (175°C). Test the oil by dropping in a small piece of dough; it should float to the surface and begin to brown after a few seconds.
  5. Take a small portion of the dough and roll it into a ball or oval shape. Carefully drop the dough into the hot oil, frying a few pieces at a time, without overcrowding the pan.
  6. Fry the doughnuts for about 3-4 minutes, turning occasionally, until golden brown and crispy on the outside.
  7. Remove the Sata Andagi from the oil and drain them on paper towels. Allow them to cool slightly before serving.
  8. Serve warm and enjoy this delicious Okinawan treat!
