About the Dish

Origin: Shimanto Eel comes from the Shimanto River in Kochi Prefecture, Japan, known for its clean, pristine waters that contribute to the eel’s delicate flavor.
Flavor: The eel has a rich, tender texture with a slightly sweet flavor, often enhanced by a savory soy-based sauce when grilled.
Cultural Significance: Shimanto Eel is a prized local delicacy in Kochi Prefecture and is celebrated for its high quality, often served as a special treat during festivals and important meals.

Ingredients

  • Shimanto eel (or any fresh eel): 1 whole eel, cleaned and filleted
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Sugar: 1 tablespoon
  • Mirin: 1 tablespoon
  • Sake: 1 tablespoon
  • Grated daikon radish (optional, for garnish)
  • Shichimi togarashi (optional, for spice)
  • Steamed white rice: 1 serving (for Unagi Don)

Instructions

  1. To make the eel glaze (kabayaki sauce), combine the soy sauce, sugar, mirin, and sake in a small saucepan. Bring to a simmer over low heat, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens slightly, about 5-7 minutes.
  2. Heat a grill or a pan on medium heat. If using a pan, lightly oil the surface.
  3. Grill or pan-fry the eel fillets, brushing them with the kabayaki sauce as they cook. Grill each side for about 3-4 minutes, ensuring the eel is cooked through and coated with the glaze.
  4. Serve the grilled eel over a bowl of steamed white rice (for Unagi Don). Optionally, garnish with grated daikon radish or a sprinkle of shichimi togarashi for a little spice.
  5. Enjoy the tender, flavorful Shimanto eel, which pairs beautifully with the savory sweetness of the kabayaki sauce and the rice.
