About the Dish

Origin: Katsuo no Tataki is a traditional dish from the Kochi Prefecture on Shikoku Island, where bonito (katsuo) fish is abundant. It is a popular way of preparing bonito in Japan.
Flavor: The dish has a combination of smoky, savory flavors from the seared bonito, complemented by the fresh, tangy taste of the dipping sauce and garnishes like garlic and ginger.
Cultural Significance: Katsuo no Tataki is a seasonal dish enjoyed particularly in spring and summer. It is often served in izakayas (Japanese pubs) and is considered a refreshing yet rich appetizer.

Ingredients

  • Bonito (katsuo) fillet: 200g, preferably fresh
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Rice vinegar: 1 tablespoon
  • Sake: 1 tablespoon
  • Garlic: 1 clove, thinly sliced
  • Ginger: 1 teaspoon, grated
  • Green onions: 2 stalks, finely chopped
  • Shiso leaves (optional): For garnish
  • Yuzu or lemon: A few slices for garnish (optional)
  • Salt and pepper: To taste
  • Sesame oil: 1 teaspoon (optional, for searing)

Instructions

  1. Heat a small amount of sesame oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Lightly sear the bonito fillet on all sides for about 1-2 minutes, keeping the center raw. The outside should have a nice char, while the inside remains tender and rare.
  2. Remove the bonito from the pan and let it rest for a few minutes. Once cooled slightly, slice the fish thinly against the grain.
  3. For the dipping sauce, mix soy sauce, rice vinegar, sake, and a pinch of salt and pepper in a small bowl.
  4. Arrange the sliced bonito on a plate and garnish with finely chopped green onions, thinly sliced garlic, grated ginger, and optional shiso leaves and citrus slices.
  5. Serve the Katsuo no Tataki with the dipping sauce on the side for dipping. Enjoy immediately while the fish is fresh and flavorful!
