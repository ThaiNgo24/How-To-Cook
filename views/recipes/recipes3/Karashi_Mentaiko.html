About the Dish

Origin: Karashi Mentaiko originates from Fukuoka, Japan, where it is a beloved dish made with spicy marinated cod roe.
Flavor: The dish is characterized by the spicy, savory flavors of the cod roe, enhanced with chili pepper, soy sauce, and other seasonings.
Cultural Significance: A popular delicacy in Fukuoka, Karashi Mentaiko is often served as a side dish or topping, especially with rice or noodles.

Ingredients

  • Cod roe (mentaiko): 200g (fresh or frozen)
  • Japanese chili powder (karashi): 1 tablespoon
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Sake: 1 tablespoon
  • Mirin: 1 tablespoon
  • Sugar: 1 teaspoon
  • Sesame oil: 1/2 teaspoon

Instructions

  1. Start by removing the cod roe from its membrane, separating the eggs into small chunks.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the chili powder, soy sauce, sake, mirin, sugar, and sesame oil until the sugar dissolves.
  3. Add the cod roe to the sauce mixture and gently toss to coat the roe with the marinade.
  4. Let the marinated roe sit for at least 30 minutes in the refrigerator to allow the flavors to develop.
  5. Serve the Karashi Mentaiko on top of rice, noodles, or as a side dish, or use it as a filling for onigiri (rice balls).
