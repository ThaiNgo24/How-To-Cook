About the Dish

Origin: Basashi originates from Kumamoto Prefecture in Japan and is a delicacy made from raw horse meat.
Flavor: The lean, tender horse meat has a rich, slightly sweet flavor with a delicate, smooth texture.
Cultural Significance: Basashi is often enjoyed as a delicacy in Japan, particularly in Kumamoto, where it is commonly served in restaurants and izakayas.

Ingredients

  • Horse meat (fresh and high-quality, ideally from a reputable butcher): 200g
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Wasabi: A small amount, for serving
  • Thinly sliced onions or green onions: For garnish
  • Ginger (grated, optional): A small amount
  • Shiso leaves (optional): For garnish

Instructions

  1. Ensure that the horse meat is fresh and of the highest quality. It should be sliced thinly to allow the delicate texture and flavor to shine.
  2. Arrange the thinly sliced horse meat on a plate, slightly fanning out the slices for an elegant presentation.
  3. Serve with small dishes of soy sauce and wasabi for dipping. Optionally, add grated ginger and thinly sliced onions or green onions as garnishes.
  4. If desired, add a few shiso leaves for additional fragrance and visual appeal.
  5. Enjoy immediately, appreciating the rich, tender flavor of the raw horse meat paired with the tangy soy sauce and wasabi.
