About the Dish

Origin: Motsunabe originated in Fukuoka, Japan, and is a popular hot pot dish made with beef or pork offal.
Flavor: The dish features a rich, savory broth with tender offal, complemented by vegetables like cabbage, garlic chives, and tofu.
Cultural Significance: A favorite winter dish in Fukuoka, Motsunabe is often enjoyed in social settings, perfect for sharing with family and friends.

Ingredients

  • Beef or pork offal (intestines): 500g, cleaned and cut into pieces
  • Japanese soy sauce: 3 tablespoons
  • Miso paste: 2 tablespoons
  • Garlic: 2 cloves, minced
  • Dashi stock: 4 cups
  • Cabbage: 1/4 head, chopped
  • Garlic chives (or green onions): A bunch, chopped
  • Tofu: 1 block, cut into cubes
  • Sake: 2 tablespoons (optional)
  • Chili peppers (optional, for spice)
  • Rice or noodles: To serve

Instructions

  1. Prepare the dashi stock by dissolving dashi powder in hot water or using homemade dashi. Pour the stock into a large pot.
  2. Add the soy sauce, miso paste, garlic, and sake (if using) to the pot. Stir well to combine and bring to a simmer.
  3. Add the cleaned and cut offal to the pot and cook for about 15-20 minutes until the meat is tender.
  4. Add the cabbage, garlic chives, and tofu to the pot, and cook for an additional 5-10 minutes until the vegetables are softened.
  5. If you like spice, add chili peppers to taste.
  6. Serve the Motsunabe hot, accompanied by steamed rice or noodles. Enjoy the rich, comforting flavors with friends and family!
