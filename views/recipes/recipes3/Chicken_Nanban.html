About the Dish

Origin: Chicken Nanban is a dish from the Kyushu region of Japan, inspired by Western-style fried chicken.
Flavor: The crispy chicken is coated in a tangy vinegar-based sauce and topped with a creamy, homemade tartar sauce.
Cultural Significance: A popular comfort food, Chicken Nanban is widely enjoyed in Japan and often served in casual eateries and restaurants.

Ingredients

  • Chicken breast (or thighs): 2 pieces, boneless and skinless
  • Flour: 1/4 cup
  • Cornstarch: 1/4 cup
  • Panko breadcrumbs: 1 cup
  • Egg: 1 (beaten)
  • Vegetable oil: For frying
  • Salt and pepper: To taste
  • Rice vinegar: 3 tablespoons
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Sugar: 1 tablespoon
  • Mirin: 1 tablespoon
  • For tartar sauce: Mayonnaise: 1/4 cup, Chopped pickles: 2 tablespoons, Chopped onions: 1 tablespoon, Lemon juice: 1 teaspoon

Instructions

  1. Season the chicken fillets with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. In a shallow dish, mix the flour, cornstarch, and panko breadcrumbs. Dip each chicken fillet into the flour mixture, ensuring it's fully coated. Then dip into the beaten egg and coat it again in the breadcrumb mixture.
  3. Heat vegetable oil in a frying pan over medium heat. Fry the chicken fillets for about 3-4 minutes per side, or until golden brown and crispy. Remove from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  4. In a separate small pan, combine the rice vinegar, soy sauce, sugar, and mirin. Heat over low heat until the sugar dissolves, then remove from heat. Pour this sauce over the cooked chicken fillets while they are still hot, allowing them to absorb the flavor.
  5. For the tartar sauce, mix the mayonnaise, chopped pickles, chopped onions, and lemon juice in a small bowl. Adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Top the chicken fillets with the prepared tartar sauce and serve with steamed rice and a side of vegetables.
