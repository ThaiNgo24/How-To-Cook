About the Dish

Origin: Sudachi Soba is popular in the Shikoku region of Japan, particularly in Tokushima Prefecture, where sudachi citrus is grown.
Flavor: The soba noodles are complemented by the refreshing, tangy flavor of sudachi citrus, which enhances the umami of the dipping sauce and adds a bright, aromatic note.
Cultural Significance: Sudachi Soba is a seasonal dish enjoyed in summer, offering a cool and light meal to help beat the heat in Japan.

Ingredients

  • Soba noodles: 200g
  • Sudachi citrus: 1-2, juiced (or substitute with yuzu if unavailable)
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Mirin: 1 tablespoon
  • Dashi: 1 cup (or dashi powder dissolved in water)
  • Sugar: 1 teaspoon (optional, depending on the sweetness of the sudachi)
  • Wasabi: A small amount (optional, for dipping)
  • Chopped green onions: For garnish
  • Shiso leaves: For garnish (optional)
  • Sesame seeds: For garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. Cook the soba noodles according to the package instructions. Once cooked, drain and rinse them under cold water to remove the starch and cool them down.
  2. To make the dipping sauce, combine the soy sauce, mirin, dashi, and sudachi juice in a small bowl. Add sugar if desired, and stir until the sugar dissolves.
  3. Place the soba noodles in a serving dish or individual bowls.
  4. Serve the dipping sauce on the side, garnished with chopped green onions, wasabi, and optionally, shiso leaves or sesame seeds.
  5. Dip the cold soba noodles into the tangy sudachi dipping sauce before eating. Enjoy the refreshing and light flavors of Sudachi Soba!
