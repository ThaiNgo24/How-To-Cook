About the Dish

Origin: Shima Tofu is a traditional Okinawan tofu, known for its firmer texture and slightly grainy consistency compared to regular tofu.
Flavor: It has a mild, nutty flavor, making it ideal for grilling, frying, or serving in soups and stews.
Cultural Significance: Shima Tofu is a staple in Okinawan cuisine, often used in local dishes and enjoyed for its unique texture and high protein content.

Ingredients

  • Shima Tofu: 1 block (can substitute with firm tofu if Shima Tofu is unavailable)
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Sesame oil: 1 tablespoon
  • Green onions: 2-3, chopped
  • Bonito flakes (optional): For garnish
  • Ginger (optional): Freshly grated, for garnish
  • Salt: A pinch, to taste

Instructions

  1. Cut the Shima Tofu into cubes or slices, depending on your preference.
  2. In a small pan, heat the sesame oil over medium heat. Lightly fry the tofu cubes or slices until golden brown on all sides, about 4-5 minutes.
  3. While the tofu is frying, prepare a simple dipping sauce by mixing the soy sauce with a pinch of salt in a small bowl.
  4. Once the tofu is crispy, remove it from the pan and place it on a serving plate.
  5. Drizzle the dipping sauce over the tofu or serve it on the side for dipping.
  6. Garnish with chopped green onions, bonito flakes, and freshly grated ginger (if using) for added flavor and aroma.
  7. Serve hot and enjoy this delicious and hearty Okinawan treat!
