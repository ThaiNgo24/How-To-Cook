About the Dish

Origin: Hakata Ramen comes from the Hakata district of Fukuoka, Kyushu, known for its rich tonkotsu pork bone broth.
Flavor: The creamy, savory broth pairs with thin, firm noodles, topped with chashu pork, green onions, and pickled ginger.
Cultural Significance: A beloved dish in Fukuoka, Hakata Ramen is widely popular across Japan for its bold flavor and comforting nature.

Ingredients

  • Pork bones (preferably neck or leg bones): 1 kg
  • Water: 2-3 liters
  • Fresh ramen noodles: 200g
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Salt: 1 teaspoon (adjust to taste)
  • Mirin: 1 tablespoon (optional, for sweetness)
  • Chashu pork (braised pork belly): 2-3 slices
  • Green onions: 2-3 stalks, chopped
  • Pickled ginger: A few slices (optional)
  • Sesame seeds: For garnish
  • Soft-boiled egg: 1, optional

Instructions

  1. Start by boiling the pork bones in a large pot of water. Bring to a boil and then reduce to a simmer. Skim off any impurities that float to the top. Simmer the bones for 6-8 hours to create a rich, creamy tonkotsu broth. Add more water as needed to keep the bones submerged.
  2. While the broth is cooking, prepare the ramen noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  3. Once the broth is ready, strain it to remove the bones and any remaining solid pieces. Season the broth with soy sauce, salt, and mirin (if using) to taste.
  4. To serve, place a portion of noodles in each bowl and pour the hot tonkotsu broth over the noodles.
  5. Top the ramen with chashu pork slices, chopped green onions, a few slices of pickled ginger, sesame seeds, and a soft-boiled egg (if using).
  6. Serve hot and enjoy the rich, savory flavors of Hakata Ramen!
