About the Dish

Origin: Naruto Kintoki Sweet Potato is grown in the Naruto region of Tokushima Prefecture, Japan. It is known for its rich sweetness and smooth texture.
Flavor: The sweet potato has a natural, deep sweetness with a creamy, soft texture that becomes more pronounced when cooked.
Cultural Significance: Naruto Kintoki Sweet Potato is highly regarded in Japan for its flavor and is often used in traditional sweets and dishes. It is a popular ingredient during the autumn and winter months.

Ingredients

  • Naruto Kintoki sweet potatoes: 2-3 medium-sized
  • Sugar: 2 tablespoons (optional, depending on sweetness)
  • Butter: 1 tablespoon (optional, for added richness)
  • Salt: A pinch (optional, to enhance sweetness)
  • Cream: 2 tablespoons (optional, for creamy texture)

Instructions

  1. Peel the Naruto Kintoki sweet potatoes and cut them into large chunks.
  2. Place the sweet potato chunks in a pot and cover them with water. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the potatoes are tender, about 15-20 minutes.
  3. Drain the water and mash the sweet potatoes with a fork or potato masher until smooth.
  4. Optional: For added richness, stir in butter, sugar, and salt to taste. If you want a creamier texture, add a little cream or milk.
  5. Serve the mashed sweet potatoes as a side dish or use them as a filling for desserts such as sweet potato cakes or pies.
