About the Dish

Origin: Nagasaki Champon originates from Nagasaki, Japan, and is a unique fusion of Chinese and Japanese culinary styles.
Flavor: The dish has a rich, savory broth made from pork and seafood, complemented by chewy noodles and a variety of vegetables and meats.
Cultural Significance: Champon is a beloved local dish in Nagasaki, often enjoyed by families and is commonly served in casual eateries throughout the city.

Ingredients

  • Ramen noodles (or champon noodles): 200g
  • Pork belly (thinly sliced): 100g
  • Shrimp (peeled and deveined): 4-5 pieces
  • Squid (sliced): 1/2 small squid
  • Cabbage (chopped): 1/4 head
  • Carrot (julienned): 1 small carrot
  • Shiitake mushrooms (sliced): 2-3 mushrooms
  • Garlic (minced): 2 cloves
  • Ginger (minced): 1 teaspoon
  • Chicken or pork broth: 4 cups
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Sake: 1 tablespoon
  • Salt and pepper: To taste
  • Sesame oil: 1 tablespoon
  • Green onions (chopped): For garnish
  • Bean sprouts (optional): For garnish

Instructions

  1. Cook the ramen noodles according to package instructions, then drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat sesame oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and ginger, and sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add the sliced pork belly and cook until browned. Then, add the shrimp and squid, cooking for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add the cabbage, carrot, and shiitake mushrooms, and stir-fry for a few minutes until the vegetables start to soften.
  5. Pour in the chicken or pork broth and bring to a simmer. Season with soy sauce, sake, salt, and pepper. Let it simmer for about 5-10 minutes until the flavors meld together.
  6. Place the cooked noodles in a bowl and pour the hot broth and toppings over the noodles.
  7. Garnish with chopped green onions and optional bean sprouts. Serve hot and enjoy!
