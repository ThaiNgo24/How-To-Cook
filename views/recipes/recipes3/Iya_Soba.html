About the Dish

Origin: Iya Soba originates from the Iya Valley in Tokushima Prefecture, Japan. It is known for its rustic, thick soba noodles made from buckwheat flour.
Flavor: The soba has a firm, hearty texture with an earthy, nutty flavor from the buckwheat, often served with a savory dipping sauce or in a hot broth with local ingredients like wild vegetables.
Cultural Significance: Iya Soba is a regional specialty in Tokushima Prefecture, deeply connected to the rural lifestyle of the Iya Valley, and it’s enjoyed as both a comforting meal and a symbol of the area's natural beauty.

Ingredients

  • Soba noodles: 200g (preferably hand-made or from Iya region)
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Mirin: 1 tablespoon
  • Dashi: 1 cup (or dashi powder dissolved in water)
  • Sugar: 1 teaspoon (optional)
  • Green onions: 2 stalks, chopped
  • Shiitake mushrooms: 3-4, sliced (optional, for added flavor)
  • Wild vegetables or mountain herbs (optional, for garnish)
  • Wasabi: A small amount (optional, for dipping)

Instructions

  1. Cook the soba noodles according to the package instructions. Once cooked, drain and rinse them under cold water to cool them down and remove excess starch.
  2. To make the dipping sauce, combine the soy sauce, mirin, dashi, and sugar in a small pot. Bring to a simmer and allow it to cook for 2-3 minutes to combine the flavors.
  3. Once the sauce is ready, remove from heat and let it cool slightly.
  4. Serve the cooked soba noodles in a bowl, and pour the dipping sauce over the noodles or serve the sauce on the side in a small dish.
  5. Garnish with chopped green onions, shiitake mushrooms, and wild vegetables or mountain herbs for added texture and flavor.
  6. Optional: Serve with a small amount of wasabi on the side for added spice. Enjoy the hearty and earthy flavors of Iya Soba!
