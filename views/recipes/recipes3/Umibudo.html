About the Dish

Origin: Umibudo is a type of seaweed native to the waters around Okinawa, Japan, known for its grape-like appearance and delicate texture.
Flavor: The seaweed has a mild, salty flavor and a unique popping texture, often enjoyed with a light dipping sauce.
Cultural Significance: Umibudo is a popular delicacy in Okinawa, often served in salads or as an appetizer, and is valued for both its unique taste and nutritional benefits.

Ingredients

  • Umibudo (sea grapes): 100g
  • Soy sauce: 1 tablespoon
  • Vinegar (preferably rice vinegar): 1 tablespoon
  • Sesame oil (optional): 1 teaspoon
  • Chili flakes or wasabi (optional): For extra spice
  • Shiso leaves (optional): For garnish

Instructions

  1. Rinse the Umibudo gently under cold water to remove any salt or impurities. Drain them well.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the soy sauce, vinegar, and sesame oil (if using) to make the dipping sauce.
  3. Place the rinsed Umibudo in a serving dish. Optionally, garnish with fresh shiso leaves for added aroma.
  4. Serve the Umibudo with the dipping sauce on the side. If desired, add a sprinkle of chili flakes or a small amount of wasabi to the sauce for an extra kick.
  5. Enjoy this refreshing, nutritious dish as a side or appetizer in your meal!
