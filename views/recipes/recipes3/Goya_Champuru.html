About the Dish

Origin: Goya Champuru comes from Okinawa, Japan, and is a traditional stir-fry dish made with goya (bitter melon).
Flavor: The dish has a savory and slightly bitter flavor from the goya, complemented by tofu, pork, and eggs, which balance the bitterness.
Cultural Significance: Goya Champuru is an iconic dish in Okinawan cuisine, known for its health benefits, especially in promoting longevity, a characteristic of the Okinawan diet.

Ingredients

  • Goya (bitter melon): 1 medium, sliced thinly
  • Pork belly or pork loin: 100g, sliced thinly (optional, can be substituted with spam or other meats)
  • Tofu (firm or silken): 1 block (about 200g), drained and cut into cubes
  • Eggs: 2, beaten
  • Soy sauce: 1 tablespoon
  • Sake: 1 tablespoon
  • Sesame oil: 1 tablespoon
  • Garlic (minced): 1 clove (optional)
  • Salt and pepper: To taste
  • Green onions (optional): For garnish

Instructions

  1. Slice the goya (bitter melon) into thin rounds and remove the seeds. Sprinkle with a pinch of salt and set aside to reduce bitterness for about 10 minutes, then rinse with water.
  2. Heat sesame oil in a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add the pork and stir-fry until browned and cooked through. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, add a little more sesame oil and sauté the garlic (if using) for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add the tofu cubes to the pan, breaking them up gently as you stir-fry. Cook for about 3-4 minutes until lightly browned and heated through.
  5. Add the sliced goya to the pan and stir-fry for 5-7 minutes, until the goya is tender but still retains some crunch.
  6. Push the ingredients to one side of the pan, and pour the beaten eggs into the empty space. Scramble the eggs until cooked through, then mix everything together.
  7. Season with soy sauce, sake, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine and ensure everything is well-coated in the seasoning.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with chopped green onions if desired, and enjoy with steamed rice.
