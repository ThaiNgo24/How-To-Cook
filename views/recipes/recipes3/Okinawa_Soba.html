About the Dish

Origin: Okinawa Soba is a specialty from Okinawa Prefecture, known for its unique noodles and savory broth.
Flavor: The dish features chewy wheat noodles in a rich and savory broth, typically made with pork or chicken, and topped with tender pork belly, fish cakes, and green onions.
Cultural Significance: Okinawa Soba is an iconic dish in Okinawa, often enjoyed as a comfort food and a part of the island's unique culinary heritage.

Ingredients

  • Okinawa Soba noodles: 2 servings (or substitute with udon noodles if unavailable)
  • Pork belly (rafute): 200g, thinly sliced
  • Chicken stock or pork broth: 4 cups
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Sake: 1 tablespoon
  • Mirin: 1 tablespoon
  • Fish cakes (chikuwa or kamaboko): 2-3 slices
  • Green onions: 2 stalks, chopped
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece, sliced thinly
  • Sesame oil: 1 teaspoon (for frying pork belly)
  • Salt and pepper: To taste

Instructions

  1. Start by preparing the broth. In a pot, bring the chicken stock or pork broth to a simmer. Add soy sauce, sake, mirin, and ginger slices. Let the broth simmer for 15-20 minutes to develop flavor.
  2. While the broth is simmering, heat sesame oil in a separate pan over medium heat. Add the pork belly slices and cook until browned and crispy on the edges. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. Cook the Okinawa Soba noodles according to the package instructions (usually by boiling them for a few minutes). Drain and set aside.
  4. Once the broth is ready, strain out the ginger slices. Add the cooked noodles into the pot and allow them to soak in the broth for 1-2 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  5. Serve the noodles in bowls, topping with the cooked pork belly, fish cakes, and chopped green onions. Optionally, garnish with more ginger or sesame seeds.
  6. Enjoy your Okinawa Soba hot, and savor the unique blend of flavors from the rich broth and tender toppings.
