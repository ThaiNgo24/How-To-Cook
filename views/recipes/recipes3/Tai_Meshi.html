About the Dish

Origin: Tai Meshi originates from the Kansai region of Japan and is often prepared for celebratory occasions.
Flavor: The rice absorbs the rich and savory flavor of the red snapper, creating a fragrant, umami-packed dish. The fish adds a delicate sweetness and depth to the rice.
Cultural Significance: Tai Meshi is a festive dish enjoyed during special occasions like New Year's or weddings, symbolizing good luck and prosperity in Japanese culture.

Ingredients

  • Red snapper (tai): 200g, filleted
  • Sushi rice: 2 cups, rinsed
  • Water: 2 1/2 cups
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Mirin: 1 tablespoon
  • Sake: 1 tablespoon
  • Salt: A pinch
  • Shiso leaves (optional): For garnish
  • Sesame seeds (optional): For garnish

Instructions

  1. Rinse the sushi rice thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear. Place the rice in a rice cooker or pot.
  2. In a bowl, combine the water, soy sauce, mirin, sake, and a pinch of salt. Pour this mixture over the rice.
  3. Place the fillets of red snapper (tai) on top of the rice. Cover the pot and cook the rice according to the rice cooker's instructions or simmer over low heat for about 20 minutes if using a pot.
  4. Once the rice is cooked, gently mix the fish into the rice, breaking it up into smaller pieces.
  5. Garnish with shiso leaves and sesame seeds for added flavor and decoration, then serve hot.
