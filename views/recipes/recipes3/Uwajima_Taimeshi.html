About the Dish

Origin: Uwajima Taimeshi comes from Uwajima City in Ehime Prefecture, Japan, where it is a beloved regional specialty.
Flavor: The dish combines the delicate, sweet flavor of red snapper with the savory rice, infused with the richness of fish broth and seasoned with soy sauce and other seasonings.
Cultural Significance: Uwajima Taimeshi is a symbol of the region's deep connection to the sea and its use of high-quality local fish, making it a popular dish for special occasions and local meals.

Ingredients

  • Red snapper (tai): 1 small whole fish, cleaned and filleted
  • Sushi rice: 2 cups, rinsed
  • Water: 2 1/2 cups
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Mirin: 1 tablespoon
  • Sake: 1 tablespoon
  • Dashi stock: 1/2 cup (or dashi powder dissolved in water)
  • Salt: A pinch (optional)
  • Chopped green onions: For garnish
  • Shiso leaves (optional): For garnish

Instructions

  1. Rinse the sushi rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Place the rice in a rice cooker or pot and add the water, soy sauce, mirin, sake, and dashi stock. Stir gently to mix the ingredients.
  2. Place the cleaned and filleted red snapper on top of the rice in the cooker or pot.
  3. Cover and cook the rice according to the rice cooker instructions, or simmer over low heat for about 15-20 minutes if using a pot. The fish will cook while the rice steams, infusing the rice with its flavor.
  4. Once cooked, gently mix the rice and fish together, breaking up the fish into smaller pieces if desired.
  5. Garnish with chopped green onions and shiso leaves, and serve hot.
