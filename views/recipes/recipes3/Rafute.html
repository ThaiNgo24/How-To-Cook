About the Dish

Origin: Rafute is a classic dish from Okinawa, Japan, known for its tender, braised pork belly cooked in a sweet and savory broth.
Flavor: The pork belly is tender and flavorful, with a rich, sweet-savory taste from the soy sauce, brown sugar, and sake braising liquid.
Cultural Significance: Rafute is a beloved dish in Okinawan cuisine, often served as a main dish or as part of Okinawa Soba, and is associated with special occasions and celebrations.

Ingredients

  • Pork belly: 500g (cut into large chunks)
  • Soy sauce: 4 tablespoons
  • Brown sugar: 3 tablespoons
  • Sake: 3 tablespoons
  • Mirin: 2 tablespoons
  • Ginger: 1-inch piece, sliced thinly
  • Garlic (optional): 2 cloves, smashed
  • Water: 3 cups
  • Green onions (optional): For garnish
  • Sesame oil: 1 teaspoon (for searing the pork belly)

Instructions

  1. Cut the pork belly into large chunks.
  2. Heat sesame oil in a pot, and sear the pork on all sides until browned (about 5-7 minutes).
  3. Add sliced ginger and garlic (if using), and stir for 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Add soy sauce, brown sugar, sake, mirin, and water. Stir to dissolve the sugar.
  5. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and cover. Simmer for 1.5-2 hours until the pork is tender.
  6. Once tender, remove from heat and let the pork sit in the broth for a few minutes.
  7. Serve hot, optionally garnished with green onions.
