About the Dish

Origin: Tonkatsu is a Japanese dish that originated in Tokyo and is influenced by Western-style breaded cutlets.
Flavor: The crispy, golden-brown crust surrounds a tender pork cutlet, often served with a sweet and tangy tonkatsu sauce.
Cultural Significance: Tonkatsu is a popular comfort food in Japan, frequently enjoyed with rice and shredded cabbage, and is often served in both casual eateries and restaurants.

Ingredients

  • Pork loin or pork fillet: 2 pieces (about 150-200g each)
  • Panko breadcrumbs: 1 cup
  • Flour: 1/2 cup
  • Egg: 1 (beaten)
  • Vegetable oil: For frying
  • Salt and pepper: To taste
  • Shredded cabbage: For serving
  • Rice: For serving
  • Tonkatsu sauce: 2-3 tablespoons (or more to taste)

Instructions

  1. Season the pork cutlets with salt and pepper on both sides.
  2. Place the flour, beaten egg, and panko breadcrumbs in separate shallow dishes.
  3. Coat each pork cutlet in flour, then dip it in the egg, and finally coat it with the panko breadcrumbs, pressing gently to adhere the breadcrumbs to the meat.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan or pot over medium heat. Once the oil reaches 170-180°C (340-350°F), carefully add the breaded pork cutlets.
  5. Fry the cutlets for about 4-5 minutes on each side until they are golden brown and crispy. Remove from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  6. Serve the tonkatsu with shredded cabbage, steamed rice, and drizzle with tonkatsu sauce. Optionally, serve with a wedge of lemon for added freshness.
