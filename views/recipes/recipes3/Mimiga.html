About the Dish

Origin: Mimiga is a traditional Okinawan dish made from boiled pig's ears, known for its chewy and crunchy texture.
Flavor: The dish is mildly flavored, often seasoned with soy sauce, vinegar, and sometimes chili for a tangy kick.
Cultural Significance: Mimiga is a popular delicacy in Okinawa, often served as a side dish or appetizer, and is considered a part of the island’s unique pork-based cuisine.

Ingredients

  • Pig's ears: 2, cleaned and trimmed
  • Vinegar: 2 tablespoons
  • Soy sauce: 2 tablespoons
  • Sesame oil: 1 teaspoon
  • Green onions: 1-2, finely chopped
  • Red chili peppers: 1-2, sliced (optional)
  • Ginger: 1 tablespoon, grated
  • Garlic: 1 clove, minced

Instructions

  1. Boil the pig's ears in a pot of water for about 1 to 1.5 hours until they become tender. Skim off any impurities during the boiling process.
  2. Once the pig's ears are tender, remove them from the water and allow them to cool slightly.
  3. Slice the pig's ears thinly into bite-sized pieces once they are cool enough to handle.
  4. In a bowl, mix the soy sauce, vinegar, sesame oil, grated ginger, and minced garlic to create the dressing.
  5. Place the sliced pig's ears into a serving dish and pour the dressing over them. Toss gently to coat the ears evenly in the sauce.
  6. Garnish with chopped green onions and red chili slices (if using) for an added kick and color.
  7. Serve the Mimiga chilled as a refreshing appetizer or side dish.
