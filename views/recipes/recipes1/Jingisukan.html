About the Dish

Origin: Introduced to Hokkaido in the 20th century.
Flavor: Marinated lamb grilled with vegetables, featuring a smoky, savory, and slightly sweet profile.
Cultural Significance: Reflects Hokkaido's unique adaptation of lamb dishes, named after Genghis Khan.

Ingredients

  • -Marinade-
  • 300ml Soy Sauce
  • 150ml Sake (Boiled to cook away alcohol)
  • Juice of 1 orange
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 60g Sugar
  • 1 Onion
  • 2 Small Apples
  • 2 Cloves Garlic
  • -Other-
  • 800g~1kg Lamb (Shoulder, Loin or Rump)
  • 1 Onion
  • 2 Green Peppers
  • 4~6 Shitake Mushrooms
  • 1/4 Japanese Squash
  • 200g Bean Sprouts
  • 200g Boiled Ramen Noodles
  • Lamb fat

Instructions

  1. Blend soy sauce, sake, orange juice, lemon juice, sugar, onion, apples, and garlic until smooth. Marinate lamb for 1-2 hours.
  2. Slice vegetables and boil ramen noodles.
  3. Rub lamb fat on a hot grill or pan.
  4. Grill marinated lamb and vegetables, flipping occasionally until cooked through.
  5. Stir-fry boiled noodles with remaining marinade on the grill for added flavor.
  6. Plate grilled lamb, vegetables, and noodles. Enjoy hot!
