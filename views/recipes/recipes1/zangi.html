About the Dish

Origin: Kushiro, Hokkaido.
Flavor: Crispy on the outside, juicy and flavorful inside, often seasoned with soy sauce and garlic.
Cultural Significance: A popular izakaya (pub) dish reflecting Hokkaido’s love for hearty comfort food.

Ingredients

  • 1kg Chicken thighs (boned)
  • 3 Tbsp Sake
  • 4 Tbsp Soy sauce
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2 Tbsp Sugar
  • 40g Ginger
  • 4 cloves Garlic
  • 1 tsp Ground white pepper
  • 1/2 tsp Ground black pepper
  • 2 eggs
  • 4 Tbsp flour
  • 10 Tbsp Katakuriko (Potato starch)

Instructions

  1. Cut chicken thighs into bite-sized pieces. Mix sake, soy sauce, salt, sugar, ginger, garlic, and both white and black pepper in a bowl. Marinate chicken for 30 minutes.
    2. Prepare Coating:
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk eggs. Combine flour and katakuriko in another bowl.
  3. Dip marinated chicken pieces in egg mixture, then coat with flour and potato starch mix.
  4. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Fry the chicken until golden and crispy, about 5-6 minutes.
    5. Serve:
  5. Drain excess oil and serve your crispy Zangi hot!
