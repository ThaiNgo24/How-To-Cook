About the Dish

Origin: Chanko Nabe, originally made for sumo wrestlers, is now widely enjoyed.
Flavor: A rich, savory broth with proteins like chicken, fish, or tofu, and vegetables.
Cultural Significance: Represents strength and endurance, a symbol of sumo culture.

Ingredients

  • Anko (sweet red bean paste): 200g (can be homemade or store-bought)
  • Tofu: 1 block, cut into cubes
  • Shiitake mushrooms: 4-5, sliced
  • Carrot: 1, sliced
  • Daikon radish: 1/2, sliced
  • Konjac (optional): 100g, cut into slices
  • Napa cabbage: 1/4, chopped
  • Green onions: 2-3 stalks, chopped
  • Vegetable or dashi broth: 4 cups
  • Soy sauce: 1-2 tablespoons (optional for added depth of flavor)
  • Sugar: 1-2 tablespoons (to balance the sweetness of the anko)
  • Salt: To taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the broth: In a large pot, bring the vegetable or dashi broth to a simmer. Add soy sauce (if using) and sugar to balance the savory-sweet flavors.
  2. Add vegetables and tofu: Add the carrots, daikon radish, shiitake mushrooms, napa cabbage, and tofu cubes. Let them cook for 10-15 minutes until tender.
  3. Add the anko: Stir in the anko (sweet red bean paste) to thicken the broth and add sweetness. Adjust with sugar if necessary.
  4. Simmer: Let everything simmer for another 10-15 minutes to meld the flavors. Add salt to taste.
  5. Serve: Garnish with chopped green onions and serve hot, either with rice or on its own.
