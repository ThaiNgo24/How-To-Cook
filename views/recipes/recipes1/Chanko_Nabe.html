About the Dish

Origin: Chanko Nabe, originally made for sumo wrestlers, is now enjoyed widely.
Flavor: A rich, savory broth with proteins like chicken, fish, or tofu, and vegetables.
Cultural Significance: Represents strength and endurance, a symbol of sumo culture.

Ingredients

  • 300g chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1/2 block tofu, cubed
  • 1/2 daikon radish, sliced
  • 1/2 carrot, sliced
  • 1/2 bunch napa cabbage, chopped
  • 1/2 bunch green onions, cut into pieces
  • 4 shiitake mushrooms
  • 3 cups dashi broth
  • 2 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp sake
  • 1 Tbsp mirin
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Soup Base: Combine the chicken broth, sake, and grated garlic in a pot over medium heat. Bring to a simmer and season with salt.
  2. Make the Meatballs: In a bowl, mix the ground chicken with salt, pepper, ginger, and green onion. Stir in potato starch. Shape the mixture into small meatballs and drop them into the simmering broth. Cook until the meatballs rise to the surface (about 5 minutes).
  3. Add Shrimp and Tofu: Add the shrimp and cut the aburaage (tofu) into small pieces. Add them to the pot and let them cook for a few minutes.
  4. Add Vegetables: Add bok choy and shiitake mushrooms to the pot. Simmer until the shrimp turn opaque and vegetables are tender, about 5 more minutes.
  5. Garnish and Serve: Drizzle with toasted sesame oil, and garnish with sliced green onion and sesame seeds before serving.
