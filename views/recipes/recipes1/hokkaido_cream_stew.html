About the Dish

Origin: Developed using local dairy products.
Flavor: Creamy, mild, and comforting with tender vegetables and meat.
Cultural Significance: A fusion dish showcasing Hokkaido's dairy and agricultural abundance.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 250 grams onion (1 medium onion, sliced 1/2-inch thick)
  • 340 grams carrots (~1 carrot cut into 1-inch pieces)
  • 370 grams potatoes (~2 medium potatoes, cut into 1.5-inch pieces)
  • 600 grams chicken thighs (cut into 2-inch pieces)
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 3 cups vegetable stock
  • 120 grams broccoli (1/2 head cut into bite-size pieces)
  • 3/4 cup heavy cream
  • 40 grams flour (5 tablespoons)
  • 1 tablespoon dried milk powder
  • 130 grams mushrooms (I used oyster mushrooms)
  • 1/2 cup creamed corn

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat vegetable oil over medium heat.
  2. Add onions, carrots, and potatoes. Sauté for 2 minutes.
  3. Add chicken thighs, salt, and white pepper. Cook until slightly browned.
  4. Add bay leaf and vegetable stock. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Simmer for 20 minutes.
  5. Blanch broccoli in boiling water for 2 minutes. Set aside.
  6. In a small bowl, whisk flour, dried milk powder, and 3/4 cup of stew liquid until smooth. Stir mixture into the pot.
  7. Simmer for 10 minutes until thickened.
  8. Stir in heavy cream, mushrooms, broccoli, and creamed corn. Cook for another 5 minutes.
  9. Serve warm with bread or rice.
