About the Dish

Origin: Monjayaki originated in Tsukishima, Tokyo, evolving from a children's snack to a popular dish.
Flavor: It has a savory taste with cabbage, seafood, and a gooey, crispy dashi-based batter.
Cultural Significance: Monjayaki is a communal dish, enjoyed with friends and family.

Ingredients

  • Flour: About 100g
  • Dashi stock: 200ml (you can use water if dashi is not available)
  • Cabbage: 100g, finely shredded
  • Shrimp (or squid, depending on preference): 100g, finely chopped
  • Pork (thinly sliced): 50-100g (optional)
  • Mizuna (Japanese greens): A little, finely chopped (optional)
  • Spring onions: 2-3 stalks, chopped
  • Ginger: A small piece, finely chopped
  • Tonkatsu sauce or Okonomiyaki sauce: 2-3 tablespoons
  • Japanese mayonnaise: A little (optional)
  • Cheese (optional): A little, grated

Instructions

  1. Prepare the batter: Mix the flour with dashi stock until smooth, making sure there are no lumps.
  2. Stir-fry ingredients: Heat a skillet and stir-fry the pork, shrimp/squid, spring onions, ginger, and cabbage until cooked.
  3. Add the batter: Pour the batter over the cooked ingredients in the skillet and mix everything together until the ingredients are evenly coated.
  4. Add sauce and seasoning: When the dish is nearly done, add the tonkatsu or okonomiyaki sauce and mix well. If you like, you can also add a bit of Japanese mayonnaise and cheese on top for extra flavor.
  5. Serve hot: Once finished, eat the monjayaki directly from the skillet while it’s hot using chopsticks or a spoon.
