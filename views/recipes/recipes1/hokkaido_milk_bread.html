About the Dish

Origin: Hokkaido, known for its high-quality dairy products.
Flavor: Soft, fluffy, and slightly sweet with a milky aroma.
Cultural Significance: A staple of Hokkaido’s dairy industry, enjoyed nationwide.

Ingredients

  • 35g (1/4 cup) bread flour
  • 175g (3/4 cup) water
  • 340g (2+2/3 cup) bread flour
  • 3g (1/2 tsp) salt
  • 30g (2 tbsp) sugar
  • 5g (1+1/4 tsp) active dry yeast
  • 40g (less than 1 medium size) egg
  • 140g (1/2 cup + 1 tbsp) whole milk, lukewarm
  • 40g (3 tbsp) unsalted butter, softened at room temperature

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix 35g bread flour with 175g water. Heat to 65°C (149°F), then let it cool. Set aside for 10-15 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, combine 340g bread flour, salt, sugar, yeast, egg, lukewarm milk, and the starter. Mix until it forms a dough.
  3. Transfer the dough to a work surface and knead for 8-10 minutes until smooth. Add softened butter and knead until fully incorporated.
  4. Place the dough in an oiled bowl, cover, and let it rise in a warm place for 1-1.5 hours, or until doubled in size.
  5. Punch the dough down and divide it into 3 equal pieces. Shape them into rolls and place them in a greased loaf pan. Cover and let it rise for 1 hour.
  6. Preheat the oven to 180°C (350°F). Bake the bread for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown on top.
  7. Let the bread cool on a wire rack before slicing and serving.
