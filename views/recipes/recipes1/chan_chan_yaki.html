About the Dish

Origin: Coastal areas of Hokkaido.
Flavor: Sweet and savory miso glaze over salmon and vegetables, cooked on a griddle.
Cultural Significance: A communal dish, showcasing Hokkaido’s fishing heritage and family dining traditions.

Ingredients

  • 2 Salmon Fillets
  • A sprinkle of Salt
  • 2 tsp Sake
  • 1/2 Onion (100g/3.53 oz)
  • 2 Cabbage Leaves (200g/7.05 oz)
  • 2 Shiitake Mushrooms
  • 50g Shimeji Mushrooms
  • 1 Potato
  • 1 Bell Pepper
  • 1/2 Long Green Onion
  • 5 tbsp Frozen Corn
  • 2 tsp Butter
  • 2 tbsp Miso
  • 2 tbsp Sake
  • 1 tsp Soy Sauce
  • 2 tsp Sugar
  • 1 tsp Grated Ginger Root
  • Vegetable Oil
  • 1 tablespoon (tbsp) = 15 ml, 1 teaspoon (tsp) = 5 ml

Instructions

  1. Sprinkle salmon fillets with salt and sake. Let rest for 10 minutes.
  2. Slice onion, cabbage, mushrooms, potato, bell pepper, and green onion into bite-sized pieces.
  3. Combine miso, sake, soy sauce, sugar, and grated ginger in a bowl.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a pan, stir-fry all vegetables until slightly tender.
  5. Place salmon fillets on top of the vegetables, cover, and steam for 5-7 minutes.
  6. Pour the sauce over the salmon and vegetables. Add butter and cook until everything is well-coated and aromatic.
  7. Plate salmon and vegetables. Enjoy warm!
