About the Dish

Origin: Ishikari River, Hokkaido.
Flavor: Rich miso broth infused with salmon, vegetables, and tofu, offering a hearty, umami-packed taste.
Cultural Significance: A traditional dish created by local fishermen to utilize abundant salmon, symbolizing Hokkaido’s seafood culture.

Ingredients

  • 2 Salmon Fillets
  • Salt
  • 1 Potato that holds its shape when cooked (ex. may queen)
  • 1~2 Cabbage or Napa Cabbage Leaves, cut into 5x5cm/2x2” pieces
  • 6cm Ear of Corn, boiled (2.4”)
  • 4 stalks of Shungiku, edible chrysanthemum greens
  • 1 Long Green Onion, cut into 3cm/1.2” pieces
  • 2 Shiitake Mushrooms
  • 50g Maitake Mushrooms (1.8 oz)(or substitute: enoki and shimeji mushrooms)
  • 200g Tofu (7.1 oz)
  • Butter, optional
  • 600ml Water (2.54 cups)
  • 10g Dried Kombu Seaweed for dashi (0.4 oz)
  • 2½ tbsp Miso
  • 2 tbsp Sake

Instructions

  1. Soak kombu in water for 30 minutes, then simmer gently for 10 minutes. Remove kombu.
  2. Add potato, cabbage, corn, green onion, mushrooms, and tofu to the broth. Simmer until vegetables are tender.
  3. Add salmon fillets to the pot and cook until just done. Add sake and miso, stirring gently.
  4. Add a pat of butter for extra richness.
  5. Dish up in bowls and enjoy hot with your favorite sides!
