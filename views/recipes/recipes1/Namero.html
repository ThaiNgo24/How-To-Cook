About the Dish

Origin: Namero originated in the Boso Peninsula, created by fishermen as a portable meal with fresh fish and seasonings.
Flavor: Minced fish combined with miso, ginger, and green onions, offering a savory, slightly sweet, and aromatic taste.
Cultural Significance: Namero reflects the resourcefulness of coastal communities, highlighting fresh, locally sourced fish.

Ingredients

  • 200g fresh fish (horse mackerel or sardines)
  • 1 Tbsp miso paste
  • 1/2 tsp ginger, grated
  • 1/2 tsp green onions, chopped
  • 1 tsp shiso leaves, chopped

Instructions

  1. Rinse the fish (horse mackerel or sardines) and cut into small pieces or shred into chunks.
  2. Wash and chop green onions and shiso leaves, then grate the ginger.
  3. Place the prepared fish into a bowl.
  4. Add 1 tablespoon of miso paste, grated ginger, chopped green onions, and shiso leaves to the bowl with the fish.
  5. Mix all the ingredients thoroughly until well combined.
  6. Enjoy immediately or let it sit in the fridge to allow the flavors to meld.
