About the Dish

Origin: Kashira Yaki is a traditional Japanese dish made with the head of tai (sea bream) or other small fish.
Flavor: Grilled fish head with a savory, salty taste, often served with grated daikon and soy sauce.
Cultural Significance: A festive dish, enjoyed in Japan during special occasions and celebrations.

Ingredients

  • 6 small fish (tai, or other small fish)
  • Salt
  • 1 daikon radish, grated
  • Soy sauce (shoyu)
  • Lemon (optional)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the fish: Clean the fish by removing the scales and internal organs.
  2. Make incisions: Make a few diagonal cuts on both sides of the fish to help the heat penetrate evenly.
  3. Season: Sprinkle salt on the fish.
  4. Grill: Grill the fish over medium heat until both sides are golden brown and cooked through.
  5. Serve: Place the grilled fish on a plate, add a spoonful of grated daikon radish, drizzle with soy sauce, and add lemon if desired.
