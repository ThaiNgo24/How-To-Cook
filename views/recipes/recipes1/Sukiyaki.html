About the Dish

Origin: Sukiyaki became popular in the Meiji Era as beef was accepted as food, becoming a classic hot pot dish.
Flavor: Thinly sliced beef is cooked in a sweet-savory broth with soy sauce, sugar, mirin, tofu, mushrooms, and vegetables.
Cultural Significance: Sukiyaki is enjoyed at celebrations and family gatherings, symbolizing communal dining and togetherness.

Ingredients

  • 300g thinly sliced beef (ribeye or sirloin)
  • 1/2 block tofu, sliced
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 1/2 carrot, sliced
  • 1/2 bunch enoki mushrooms
  • 1/2 bunch shiitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 bunch green onions, cut into 5 cm pieces
  • 1 pack shirataki noodles
  • 2 cups dashi broth
  • 3 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 Tbsp mirin
  • 2 Tbsp sake
  • 1 Tbsp sugar
  • 1 egg (per person, optional for dipping)

Instructions

  1. Make the Warishita Sauce: Cook mirin and sake to burn off alcohol, then add soy sauce, sugar, and water. Simmer until the sugar dissolves. Let it soak with kombu for extra flavor.
  2. Prepare the Ingredients: Slice beef thinly and cut vegetables (shiitake mushrooms, spring onions, tofu, napa cabbage, etc.) into presentation-friendly pieces.
  3. Heat the Sukiyaki Pot: Rub the pot with beef fat and char the spring onions for extra flavor.
  4. Cook the Ingredients: Add the warishita sauce, vegetables, and tofu to the pot. Bring it to a boil, lower the heat, and add the beef.
  5. Simmer: Cook for a few minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove cooked ingredients as they are done and serve.
